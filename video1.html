Key Concepts

Manipulator (Robot Arm)

A robot arm is called a manipulator, made of solid parts called links (like bones) and moving parts called joints (where movement happens).

Joints & Links

Joints are the only parts that actually move and make the robot arm rotate or bend. Links give the arm its fixed length, just like bones in our body.

Base & Gripper

The base keeps everything stable (like the shoulder). The gripper is the robot's "hand" used to hold and move objects.

Forward Kinematics (FK)

Forward Kinematics predicts where the robot's hand will be when we set the joint angles. It uses equations to calculate the exact position (x, y coordinates).
