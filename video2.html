Key Concepts

Forward Kinematics

The process of calculating the position of a robot's hand (end-effector) when we already know the joint angles. We give joint movement as input, and FK tells us where the hand ends up.

Two-Link Robot Arm

Trigonometry in FK

Cosine helps find movement along the X-axis (horizontal), Sine helps find movement along the Y-axis (vertical). By adding contributions from both links, we calculate exact position.

Curved Path Motion

The robot hand doesn't move in a straight line - it follows a curved path because both joint angles combine together to determine the final position.
